I spent my adult life in the retail sector at the microbusiness (less than 10 employees) level. One of the few equalizers to larger, corporate outfits was the application of online sales. Watching the impact of online sales unfold in real time was highly informative. Unfortunately, outside circumstances caused the business to close, which led me to look for a new career.

My web development journey began about a year ago, after my company closed its doors. I enrolled in a coding Bootcamp. Zero previous experience with web development led to a difficult 6 months at camp, but also a curiosity I now welcome and cultivate daily. I've learned that moments of confusion are in fact parts of the journey, and moreso, markers of progress.

I believe the prospects of mobile-development are very encouraging. The affordability of technology has generated a global, egalitarian phenomenon--there are 3 billion smartphone owners worldwide. Information-sharing should sustain its continuous renaissance via mobile applications, creating a better informed, tighter connected, and increasingly empathetic global population. Markets will continue to greatly benefit from mobile development's growth potentials, at both enterprise and personal/social levels. I couldn't imagine a better place to be.
